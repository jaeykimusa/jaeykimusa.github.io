Student-led development of a six-degrees-of-freedom robotic arm with autonomous manipulation capabilities, featuring computer vision and intelligent control systems.

The field of robotic manipulation is undergoing rapid transformation with the integration of high-performance control systems, lightweight actuators, and intelligent vision-based perception. Autonomous robotic manipulators that can see, think, and act independently are becoming foundational technologies across numerous industriesâ€”including logistics, manufacturing, service robotics, and medical applications.

At North Carolina State University, our student-led research team has developed a six-degrees-of-freedom robotic arm using stepper motors and a DC-motor-powered gripper. We are actively designing and implementing a high-level controller to autonomously complete tasks such as object detection, grasping, and relocation.

To overcome current limitations, we propose transitioning to brushless DC (BLDC) motors paired with Field-Oriented Control (FOC) drivers. We also plan to integrate a Luxonis OAK-4 S camera for AI-enabled RGB-D vision and real-time object detection.

Re-Battery Recycling Program

Led a statewide sustainability initiative repurposing 14,000+ used batteries; installed custom recycling bins at major universities and schools across North Carolina.

As Founder and President, I launched and led a sustainability initiative focused on repurposing used batteries and raising awareness about proper e-waste disposal. Motivated by the environmental impact of improperly discarded batteries, I assembled and led a team to design, manufacture, and distribute custom battery recycling bins. These bins were placed at North Carolina State University, UNC Chapel Hill, and six high schools across Pitt County.

To date, the program has collected and repurposed over 14,000 batteries, providing a scalable and accessible disposal solution for local communities. In addition to operations, I oversaw outreach efforts, collaborated with school administrators and university facilities teams, and secured support from local organizations. This initiative continues to grow and serve as a model for student-led sustainability efforts in the region.
