Contact Timing Optimization for 3D Jumping on Legged Robots

Contact timing optimization using single rigid body dynamics for adaptive and efficient quadruped jumping maneuvers.

Contact timing optimization determines the optimal moments for a robot to initiate and break ground contact during dynamic maneuvers like jumping. In this work, a reduced-order model based on single rigid body (SRB) dynamics was used to solve for phase durations and center-of-mass trajectories. These results inform a full-body trajectory optimization, enabling efficient generation of physically consistent motions while significantly reducing computational cost.

This approach is essential for adaptive contact planning, allowing the robot to adjust its contact sequences in response to task demands and environmental changes. By optimizing contact timing instead of relying on fixed schedules, the robot achieves more robust, stable, and versatile behaviorsâ€”key for executing agile motions in complex, real-world settings.
