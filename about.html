About Me

Getting to know Jaey Kim

Who I Am

I'm a passionate Mechanical Engineering student at North Carolina State University with minors in Computer Science and Entrepreneurship. My journey combines the precision of engineering with the innovation of technology and the vision of entrepreneurship.

What drives me is the intersection of robotics, sustainability, and real-world problem solving. I believe that the future of engineering lies in creating intelligent systems that not only perform efficiently but also contribute to a more sustainable world.

My Journey

My fascination with robotics began early, but it was during my time at NC State that I discovered how mechanical engineering principles could be enhanced with computer science and entrepreneurial thinking. This interdisciplinary approach has shaped my perspective on creating solutions that matter.

Through hands-on research projects, particularly in robotic manipulation and locomotion systems, I've learned that the best engineering solutions come from understanding both the technical challenges and the broader impact on society and the environment.

My Passions

  • Robotics and Autonomous Systems: Designing intelligent machines that can adapt and learn
  • Locomotion Research: Understanding how movement can be optimized in biological and robotic systems
  • Sustainability: Creating engineering solutions that protect our environment
  • Innovation and Entrepreneurship: Turning ideas into impactful solutions

Beyond Engineering

When I'm not working on robotics projects or studying, you'll find me exploring new technologies, learning about sustainable design practices, or brainstorming innovative solutions to everyday problems. I believe that the best engineers are also curious about the world around them.

I'm always excited to connect with fellow engineers, researchers, and innovators who share a passion for creating technology that makes a positive difference in the world.

Let's Connect

I'm always open to discussing research opportunities, collaborative projects, or simply sharing ideas about the future of robotics and sustainable engineering. Feel free to reach out through any of the contact methods on my main page.
